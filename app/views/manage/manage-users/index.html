Research summary

Users need clearer and more relevant notifications. They prefer 'receive notifications' to 'subscribe' - although they understood both terms. Shared mailboxes are used. Findings also suggest notifications should support communication between LPA and case officer roles.

Related research taken from MiPins and Camerons's user tests with LPAs

  • “We received an auto notification of appeal to our admin inbox but it was not clear that it was enforcement (it had no enforcement notice reference on it, merely a planning application reference) so it went to the wrong department.”
  • “The number of email updates is obtrusive and focuses on the wrong elements of the process. We don't need constant email updates about when the Council have submitted the questionnaire or the deadline to do so, we do need clear direct correspondence with Case Officers managing the appeal.”
  • “Why do we not get notification that interested party comments are available. Without information about who has made representation we will not be able to include anyone who didn't comment previously into further notifications about hearing dates etc.”
  • “different people take responsiability at different times e.g. admins are for junior staff nitty gritty would be delt with by a planning officer”
  • “subscribe follow, receive email notifications - I understand all of these [terms]. Subscriptions make me think of magazines but I understood what that means”
  • “Receive notifications is probably the clearest [wording]”
  • “I would have thought anybody who commented on the initial application would get an email”
  • “I think it would go to an admin chap who receives the appeals and then he sends on to relevant case officer, it's a useful way of doing it because if the officer is on leave then he can forward it to the manager”
  • “Everyone deals with all different types of applications, more senior people deal with more difficult applications”
