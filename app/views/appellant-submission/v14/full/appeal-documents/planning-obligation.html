What is a planning obligation?

A planning obligation is when you agree to do something if your development goes ahead. This is usually to help manage your development's impact on the surrounding area.

For example, you might agree to:

  • donate to a wildlife charity, if your development will affect local wildlife
  • help fund improvements to nearby roads, if your development will increase traffic

A planning obligation is a legal document. When the agreement is between you and the local planning department, it's called a 'section 106 agreement'. When the agreement is between you and another party, it's called a 'unilateral undertaking'.
