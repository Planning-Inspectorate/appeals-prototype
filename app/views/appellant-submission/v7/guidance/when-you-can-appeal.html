When you can appeal

You can appeal a householder planning decision from your local planning department if you think all their reasons for refusing your planning application are incorrect.

You must have made the original planning application yourself. If you applied on behalf of a business or organisation, the appeal should be in the name of that organisation.

There’s a different process to appeal a full planning decision or a listed building appeal.​

You cannot use this service to appeal a high hedges decision or a tree preservation consent order decision.​

Deadline for appealing​

You must appeal within 12 weeks of the decision date on the letter from your local planning department.​

If you’ve received an enforcement notice, you must appeal within 4 weeks of the date on the notice through the main appeals service.​
