Your appeal

Sensitive information

We only collect sensitive information to the extent it is relevant to an appeal.

Your appeal statement should set out why the local planning department's decision was incorrect. You should refer to the policies they used in their decision, and their development plan.

You may choose to include sensitive information in your appeal statement. Think carefully about whether any sensitive information you provide is necessary and relevant to your case.

Your appeal and appeal statement will be shared with your local planning department.

What is sensitive information?

Use of language and sensitive information

You should not use racist, inflammatory or abusive language, or include sensitive information (also called 'special category information') about yourself or others in your statement.

If you do, you will be asked to resubmit and this will delay your appeal (or invalidate it, if you do not resubmit in time).

Types of sensitive information

Sensitive information refers to:

  • comments from children
  • information relating to children
  • information relating to health
  • information relating to crime

It also means any information relating to an individual's:

  • race
  • ethnic origin
  • politics
  • religion
  • trade union membership
  • genetics
  • physical characteristics
  • sex life
  • sexual orientation
