How to appeal

You appeal using the appeal a planning decision service.

You must submit a complete appeal by 23.59pm on the day of your deadline. Find out about your deadline to appeal.

An appeal is complete when it has all the documents and information the Planning Inspectorate needs to progress your appeal.

Your appeal statement

Your appeal statement is the main part of your appeal. This is where you explain your reasons for appealing.

You can only submit this once, so it's important to get it right.

How to write your appeal statement

You should prepare this in a document to upload with your appeal.

If you're appealing a refusal

You must explain why all the reasons for refusal are incorrect, based on planning policy.

You can find the reasons for refusal on your decision letter. Each reason for refusal includes the relevant planning policy and document numbers.

The Planning Officer's report explains how the local planning department has applied the policies.

For your appeal to be successful, the Planning Inspectorate must agree that all the reasons for refusal are incorrect.

If you're appealing conditions

You must explain why the conditions are either:

  • unnecessary
  • irrelevant to planning
  • irrelevant to your development
  • unenforceable
  • vague
  • unreasonable

If you're asking for the conditions to be changed, instead of removed, you should say what you want them to be changed to.

If you're appealing a late planning decision

You must explain why your planning application should be granted, based on planning policy.

You should focus on the parts of your application that your local planning department is likely to disagree with, if you know what these are.

Respond to comments from interested parties

You can include responses to comments from interested parties in your appeal statement. You only need to do this if they relate to reasons for appealing.

Supporting documents

You can also include other documents that support your appeal. For example, independent reports, updated plans and drawings, and photographs.

If you mention supporting documents in your statement, you must submit them with your appeal. If you do not, it will delay your appeal and you might miss your deadline.

Draft statement of common ground

A statement of common ground says what you and the local planning department agree on. You must upload a draft of this with your appeal.

How to write your draft statement of common ground

You should draft your statement using the statement of common ground pro forma.

You should say what you believe your local planning department will agree with you about. After you submit your appeal, you work with your local planning department to finalise and agree the statement.
