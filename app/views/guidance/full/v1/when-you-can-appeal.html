When you can appeal

Refused planning application

If your planning application has been refused for reasons you think are incorrect, you can appeal against the refusal.

A reason for refusal is incorrect when it goes against the local planning department's development plan and planning policies.

Granted planning permission with conditions

If your planning application has been granted with conditions you think are incorrect, you can appeal against the conditions.

A condition is incorrect when it's:

  • unnecessary
  • irrelevant to planning
  • irrelevant to your development
  • unenforceable
  • vague
  • unreasonable

You can appeal to have the conditions removed from your planning permission, or to change the conditions.

No decision by the local planning department’s deadline

If you have not got a decision on your planning application by your local planning department’s deadline, you can appeal against the late decision.

The local planning department’s deadline is usually 8 weeks from the day it acknowledged your application. But it can be longer if you agreed a different deadline.

Who can appeal

You can only appeal if you either:

  • made the planning application in your name
  • have permission to act on behalf of the person or organisation whose name is on the application

Your deadline to appeal

Appealing refusal or conditions

Your deadline is 6 months from the decision date on the letter from your local planning department.

Your deadline is sooner if you're appealing a refused householder planning application.

You made a householder planning application if you applied for:

  • householder planning permission
  • permission to vary or remove a condition on householder planning permission
  • prior approval: larger home extension
  • prior approval: enlargement of a dwellinghouse by construction of additional storeys

Find out more in the appeal a householder planning decision guidance.

Appealing if you have not got a decision by the local planning department’s deadline

Your deadline is 6 months from the date your decision was due.

If you have an enforcement notice

Your deadline may be sooner if you have an enforcement notice for the same development as your planning application - or a very similar one.

If you got the enforcement notice before your application was refused, your deadline is 28 days from the decision date on the letter from your local planning department.

If you got the enforcement notice after your application was refused, your deadline is whichever is sooner from the following:

  • 28 days from the date you received the enforcement notice
  • 6 months from the decision date on the letter from your local planning department
