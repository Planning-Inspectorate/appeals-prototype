Your appeal decision

A Planning Inspector uses evidence from you, the local planning department and other interested parties to decide your appeal.

They can decide to:

  • allow your appeal
  • reject your appeal
  • make a split decision

Appeals against refusal or a late planning decision

If your appeal is allowed

Your planning application is granted. You can go ahead with the planned work.

If your appeal is rejected

Your application stays refused.

You can still talk to your local planning department about resubmitting your application. There may be small changes you can make to improve the chances of your application being granted.

If you get a split decision

Part of your planning application is granted.

You may choose to go ahead with that part of the planned work.

You can also talk to your local planning department about resubmitting your application. There may be small changes you can make to improve the chances of your whole application being granted.

Appeals against conditions

If your appeal is allowed

Any conditions you wanted to remove no longer apply to your planning permission. Any conditions you wanted to change are changed.

If your appeal is rejected

The conditions still apply to your planning permission.

If you get a split decision

The Planning Inspector agrees with some of the conditions you want to remove or change. This could mean they:

  • change a condition instead of removing it
  • change a condition in a different way to how you wanted

If you think your decision is incorrect

If you think the Planning Inspectorate made a legal mistake, you can challenge the decision in court.

Find out more about the Administrative Court.

Get advice from a lawyer if you're unsure about this. You can find a solicitor on The Law Society's website.

If you want to complain

You can complain about how the Planning Inspectorate handled your appeal. There's no time limit for complaints.

Find out about the Planning Inspectorate's complaints procedure.
