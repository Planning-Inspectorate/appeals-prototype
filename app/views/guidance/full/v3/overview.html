Overview

This guidance is for people who want to appeal most types of planning decision. This includes if you have not got a decision by the local planning department's deadline.

You need different guidance if you want to appeal a refused application for:

  • householder planning permission
  • permission to vary or remove a condition on householder planning permission
  • prior approval: larger home extension
  • prior approval: enlargement of a dwellinghouse by construction of additional storeys

Go to the appeal a householder planning decision guidance.

If your appeal is about listed building consent, you need the appeal a listed building consent decision guidance.

Appealing to the Planning Inspectorate

You make your appeal to the Planning Inspectorate.

The Planning Inspectorate is an independent and impartial government body. It decides planning appeals on behalf of the Secretary of State.

There's no fee for appealing.
