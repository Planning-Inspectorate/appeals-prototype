Comment on a planning appeal

Anyone can comment on a planning appeal.

The Planning Inspectorate uses your comments to help it make a decision.

How to comment on an appeal

To submit your comments, find the appeal online.

If you cannot use the online service, you can submit your comments to the Planning Inspectorate's customer support team:

Email: enquiries@planninginspectorate.gov.uk

We aim to respond within 5 working days.

Telephone: 0303 444 5000

Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm (except public holidays)

Find out about call charges

Deadline to submit comments

Your deadline is either:

  • 5 weeks after the start date of the appeal
  • 6 weeks after the date on the local planning department's enforcement notice

If you commented on the planning application

The local planning department will forward your original comments to the Planning Inspectorate. You do not need to resubmit your comments.

Withdraw your original comments

If you want to withdraw your original comments, email enquiries@planninginspectorate.gov.uk

If you withdraw your comments, the Planning Inspectorate will not use your comments to help it make a decision.

Taking part in a planning appeal

Commenting is one way you can take part in a planning appeal.

You can also:

  • ask the Planning Inspectorate to view the appeal site from your property
  • ask to attend the hearing, if there is one
  • ask to attend the inquiry, if there is one

Read the detailed guidance about taking part in an appeal.
